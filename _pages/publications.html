My research focuses on how mental health systems can better promote recovery and equity, particularly for people with serious mental illness and for racial and ethnic minority communities. I examine drivers of mental health service utilization and continuity of care, including stigma and discrimination, help-seeking, and culturally and linguistically appropriate services.

Central inquiry
Re-envisioning mental health systems
around lived experience and cultural equity
Practitioner level

Recovery-oriented practice

How values become recovery-promoting competencies.

Population level

Service access & equity

Stigma, language, help-seeking among marginalized groups.

System level

System design & implementation

Peer support in mobile crisis and emergency response.

Methodological foundation
Quantitative and computational approaches:
large-scale observational data, text-as-data
